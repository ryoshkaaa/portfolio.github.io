Science projects

Over the past two years, I have worked on three STEM projects that have significantly shaped my academic and professional aspirations. These projects allowed me to gain valuable hard and soft skills while solidifying my passion for science and innovation.

1. Multilingual Affect Lexicon

This project aimed to create a Multilingual Affect Lexicon for machine learning, based on the Plutchik Wheel of Emotions, categorizing terms into eight primary emotions at three intensity levels. The lexicon supports automated emotion detection and visualization in multilingual texts, covering English, French, Russian, and Chinese.

Under the supervision of Professor Victor Sheftel, I focused on the Russian, Chinese, and English components of the lexicon. My responsibilities included:
- Selecting and evaluating words, organizing them into structured tables.
- Assisting in the overall project design and structure.
- Testing its functionality using Python.

This project enriched my understanding of scientific design, improved my technical skills, and even helped me enhance my proficiency in Chinese. Project enhances the emotional understanding of texts in multiple languages, thus, can be used in marketing, social media analysis, and customer feedback systems.

Njord: Vertical Wind Turbine for Cargo Ships

This project introduced a functional prototype of an innovative vertical wind turbine for cargo ships, addressing critical issues like global warming, pollution, and renewable energy production. The project, developed at Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology's (MIPT's) summer school, is groundbreaking in its unique design and application.

As the team leader, I coordinated a group of four, taking charge of:
- Inventing the wind generator’s unique design.
- Creating and printing 3D models using NX software, a 3D printer, and a 3D pen.
- Soldering electrical components and assembling mechanical parts.
- Presenting and documenting the project in a scientific format.

This experience honed my leadership and technical skills, particularly in teamwork, 3D modeling, and project presentation.

3. Search and Rescue Drone for Disaster Relief

My first research endeavor in robotics focused on designing an autonomous drone for disaster relief. The prototype aims to locate and rescue individuals trapped under rubble following natural disasters or armed conflicts.

During this project, I:
- Learned to independently set and solve complex design tasks.
- Developed expertise in Arduino and C++ programming.
- Assembled and soldered electronic components while integrating AI concepts.

This project sparked my interest in artificial intelligence and robotics, fields I’m eager to explore further.
